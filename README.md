# Online Retail Customer Analysis and Churn Prediction

End-to-end data analysis project combining customer segmentation, pricing analysis, and churn prediction to generate actionable business insights.

## Project Overview

This project analyzes online retail transaction data to understand customer behavior, identify key revenue drivers, and predict customer churn.

The analysis combines customer segmentation, pricing patterns, and predictive modeling to support data-driven retention strategies.

## Key Highlights

- Customer segmentation based on value and behavior  
- Pricing analysis revealing volume-driven sales patterns  
- Churn prediction model with ~67% accuracy  
- Threshold optimization for flexible retention strategies  

---

## 1. Data Understanding

Initial exploration of dataset structure, missing values, and data types.

### Insight
The dataset contains transactional records with missing customer identifiers and high variability in price and quantity, requiring careful preprocessing.

---

## 2. Data Preparation

- Removed missing Customer_ID values  
- Filtered invalid transactions (negative quantity, zero price)  
- Removed duplicates  
- Excluded non-product and return-related records  

### Insight
Data cleaning ensured that the analysis focuses only on valid customer purchases and reliable sales data.

---

## 3. Feature Engineering

- Created time-based features (Year, Month, Quarter)  
- Generated revenue metric (Price × Quantity)  
- Built behavioral features (frequency, activity period)  
- Applied revenue capping to reduce the effect of outliers  

### Insight
Feature engineering improved the ability to capture customer behavior and reduced noise from extreme values.

---

## 4. Sales Analysis

- Analyzed relationships between price, quantity, and revenue  
- Examined country-level sales distribution  
- Identified top-performing products  
- Analyzed order patterns and sales trends  

### Insight
Sales are primarily volume-driven, with revenue strongly linked to purchase quantity. A small number of products generate a large share of total revenue, and the UK market dominates sales.

---

## 5. Product-Level Insights

- Identified top products by revenue and quantity  
- Compared price vs quantity performance  
- Analyzed product distribution across markets  

### Insight
Revenue is mainly generated by frequently purchased low-to-mid priced products, while high-priced items contribute less to overall performance.

---

## 6. Customer Value Segmentation

Customers were segmented into:
- Low Value  
- Mid Value  
- High Value  
- Top Value  

### Insight
Top Value customers contribute approximately 51% of total revenue, indicating a strong concentration of value among a small customer group.

---

## 7. Price Analysis by Customer Segment

- Created price bands  
- Analyzed revenue distribution across segments  
- Compared purchasing behavior by segment  

### Insight
Revenue across all customer segments is concentrated in mid price ranges, especially between 1–5. Even high-value customers rely on moderate price levels, confirming a volume-based model.

---

## 8. Churn Modeling and Threshold Analysis

### Model
- Logistic Regression (with class balancing)

### Performance
- Accuracy: approximately 67%  
- Balanced precision and recall  
- Moderate ability to identify at-risk customers  

### Feature Importance
- Behavioral features (purchase frequency and activity) are the strongest predictors  
- Revenue alone is less effective for predicting churn  

### Threshold Analysis
- Lower thresholds increase recall (more customers flagged)  
- Higher thresholds increase precision (more accurate targeting)  

### Insight
Threshold tuning enables flexible retention strategies, allowing businesses to balance between broader outreach and more targeted actions.

---

## Key Findings

- Revenue is highly concentrated among a small group of customers and products  
- The business follows a volume-driven model  
- Mid-priced products dominate revenue generation  
- Customer behavior is more predictive of churn than monetary value  

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how transactional data can be transformed into actionable insights. By combining customer segmentation, pricing analysis, and churn prediction, the analysis provides a strong foundation for targeted retention strategies and data-driven decision-making.
